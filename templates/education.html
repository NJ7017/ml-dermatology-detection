Skin Health Education

Learn about common skin conditions and proper skin care

Acne

Common skin condition when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells.

Symptoms:
  • Whiteheads
  • Blackheads
  • Pimples

Eczema

Condition that makes skin red and itchy. Common in children but can occur at any age.

Symptoms:
  • Dry, sensitive skin
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Severe itching

Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer that develops in melanocytes.

Warning Signs (ABCDE):
  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Border irregularity
  • Color changes
  • Diameter >6mm
  • Evolving

Psoriasis

Chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid skin cell buildup.

Symptoms:
  • Red patches with silvery scales
  • Dry, cracked skin
  • Itching or burning

Sun Protection

  • Use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
  • Seek shade between 10am-4pm
  • Wear protective clothing and hats

Skin Care Basics

  • Gentle cleansing twice daily
  • Moisturize immediately after bathing
  • Avoid harsh scrubs and hot water

Healthy Lifestyle

  • Stay hydrated
  • Eat antioxidant-rich foods
  • Manage stress levels

Important: Always consult a dermatologist for proper diagnosis and treatment plans.

Topical Treatments

Creams, ointments, and lotions applied directly to the skin.

Examples: Retinoids, corticosteroids, antibiotics

Oral Medications

Prescription drugs for more severe cases.

Examples: Antibiotics, retinoids, biologics

Light Therapy

Controlled exposure to ultraviolet light under medical supervision.
